Lately, I have been traveling to different states.

It is a broad update, but it belongs here. This blog is not only a record of projects and technical work. It is also a place to keep track of the parts of life that happen between those things, and recently that has included spending time in different parts of the country.

Crossing a state line can be a small event on a map, but every trip creates a new set of details to notice. Roads change, landscapes shift, and familiar routines have to adjust to a different place. Even when the destination is not far away, leaving the usual surroundings makes the differences easier to see.

Travel also creates a different sense of time. A normal week can blend together, while a trip tends to separate itself into departures, stops, arrivals, and the moments that happen along the way. Those pieces make useful markers when looking back at what I have been doing outside of work.

I have not listed the states, dates, routes, or people involved because I have not shared those details yet. If there is a particular trip worth expanding on, it can become its own post later. For now, this note records the simple version: I have been getting out, crossing some state lines, and seeing more places.

Some updates are about what I built. This one is about where life has been taking me.
