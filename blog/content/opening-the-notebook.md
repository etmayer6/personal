I wanted a place on this site for things that do not fit neatly into a project card. A portfolio is useful for showing finished work, but it tends to skip the experiments, wrong turns, small improvements, and ordinary weeks that explain how the work actually happened.

This blog is meant to keep those details from disappearing. It will be a lightweight log of whatever seems worth recording: changes to the site, progress on side projects, something new I learned, or an occasional note from outside the editor.

There is intentionally no publishing schedule. When I have something to share, I will give Codex the rough facts and context. It can turn those notes into a first-person draft, add the post to the site, and rebuild the archive. The source remains plain Markdown, so the process stays simple and the writing stays portable.

The useful constraint is that the posts still have to sound like me. They should be direct, specific, and honest about what is finished and what is still in progress. Codex can handle the blank page and the formatting, but it should not invent a cleaner story than the one I actually give it.

For now, this is the first marker in the notebook. The next entry can be about whatever happens next.
